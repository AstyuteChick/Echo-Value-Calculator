Character Data

ER Target

  • The character needs roughly amount of ER without being buffed by Zhezhi or Yangyang's outros.

  • This represents a comfortable ER amount, which may be around 3 to 5% ER more than their actual high-estimate ER requirements. This is to account for ability misses and the value of having smooth rotations.

  • Deviations (from the actual values in practice) in range of 5, 10, 15 and more are acceptable for characters with ER importance of Max, Vital, Norm etc. respectively, as these have minimal affect on the EVC score.

  • For more details on how the target ER is considered and how it affects the EVC, check out the section on Energy Regen.

ER Importance

  • This represents the “importance” of pressing the Liberation button for a character. While it is a quantitative data type, to avoid confusion a tier system has been used.

  • “ER importance” can specifically represent, based on the context of the character, one or multiple of the following:

    • How vital it is for the character’s kit to burst every rotation.

    • The loss of damage/value or the consequences if the character doesn’t press the Liberation button in a rotation.

    • The value of an average ER substat roll relative to the value of an average Crit substat roll.

  • A rough estimate is more than sufficient. Just considering this metric already drastically improves the accuracy of the EVC; deviations on the quantitative data have no practical effect on the EVC score (for more details, check out the Energy Regen page):

    • “Min”: Pressing the Liberation button has minimal impact on the character’s effectiveness, or energy regen is not required at all.

    • “Norm”: Pressing the Liberation button is part of the character’s standard rotations, and significantly contributes in the character’s value.

    • “Vital”: Pressing Liberation is very important either due to majority of the damage and/or character value locked behind it

    • “Max”: Nearly all of the character’s value is locked behind using their liberation, resulting in ER being almost as good as crit, if not better.

Attributes (Basic/Heavy/Skill/Liberation)

  • This value represents the percentage of this type of damage the character does in a rotation, as used by the calculator.

  • Having this value accurate to a range of ±10 will have minimal to practically insignificant impact on the EVC’s results (for more details on why this is the case, check out the “Achieved Value” section).
